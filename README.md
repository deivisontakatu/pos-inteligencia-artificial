# Postgraduate Program Overview

This postgraduate program provides advanced theoretical and practical training to prepare graduates for professional practice, research, and leadership roles in the chosen field. Typical duration is 1–2 years (full-time) depending on program structure. Key objectives include development of research skills, advanced domain knowledge, critical thinking, and project management. Completion requires coursework and a capstone project or thesis.

## Subjects

| No. | Subject | Description |
|-----:|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Research Methods and Design | Advanced qualitative and quantitative research techniques, study design, and literature synthesis. |
| 2 | Advanced Theory and Practice | In-depth coverage of core theories and their practical applications in the discipline. |
| 3 | Data Analysis and Statistics | Statistical modelling, data processing, and interpretation using contemporary tools. |
| 4 | Specialized Elective I | Focused elective covering a current specialized topic within the field. |
| 5 | Specialized Elective II | Secondary elective allowing deeper exploration of a niche area. |
| 6 | Professional Skills and Leadership | Project management, communication, ethics, and leadership competencies for professionals. |
| 7 | Ethics, Policy and Compliance | Regulatory frameworks, ethical considerations, and policy implications relevant to practice. |
| 8 | Seminar Series and Critical Review | Regular seminars, guest lectures, and critical appraisal of contemporary research. |
| 9 | Capstone Project / Thesis | Independent research project culminating in a thesis or applied capstone deliverable. |
